Prediction accuracy & wind drift collected continuously · not auto-applied yet

Two passive data streams. Postmortem compares each finished transit candidate's tightest projected sep against the predicted sep at ~90 s / ~30 s / ~10 s of remaining lead — bucketed so you can read "at lead X, how far off does my prediction tend to be". Drift sampler runs every tick over every aircraft ≥ 20° elevation and reports the mean ground-track residual = today's systematic wind + ATC bias. Auto-application to extrapolate() is intentionally OFF until the numbers look stable; this panel shows the raw data.

Why the < 10 s bucket fills slowly: ADS-B itself almost never drops out (most flights emit ~30-60 squitters/min throughout reception). What drops out is the transit candidate — the moment a flight's projected path drifts outside the 2° panel band (course tweak by ATC, wind shift, slight altitude change) the tracker stops emitting it and the lifecycle entry goes stale-faded. The aircraft is still in dump1090, just no longer on a Sun/Moon-bound trajectory in our prediction window. 80-90 % of candidates fade this way before reaching imminent stage; only the <1 in 5 that hold their track all the way to closest approach contribute to the 30-60 s and <10 s buckets — those are your imminent-confirmed history rows.

Twilight appulses (aircraft × bright planet) Detect an aircraft passing near a bright planet (Venus/Jupiter/Saturn/Mars) with both inside your FOV, during twilight — a wide-field "plane next to the evening star" shot, shown in the Twilight appulses panel. Uses your real telescope FOV (from Telescope & sensor), loosened by the factor below. Off by default.
Sky targets (satellite × DSO passes) Predict when a satellite (ISS / Hubble / Tiangong) crosses the framed field of a deep-sky object, star or planet — shown in the Sky-target plan panel at the top of the page. For the "shoot the satellite, composite with a separate background stack" workflow. The object catalogue lives in config/service.json under iss.skyTargets.objects (bright stars, a few Messier, the bright planets by default; add your own by J2000 RA/Dec or a planet name). These controls tune the scan.
Plan alerts (Pushover)
A heads-up the first time a planned pass reaches the confidence below — "this transit is firming up". Separate from the live transit alerts, opt-in, and fires once per event (restart-safe). Needs Pushover configured on the Pushover tab.
Sky-target catalogue This is just the candidate pool — the objects worth scanning, not a schedule. When each is actually catchable (object up, sky dark, satellite sunlit) is decided by the Sky-target plan, which never shows daytime events; an object listed here only appears in the plan at a sensible dark-sky time (so M31 won't show up at noon). Each is a star / DSO by J2000 RA (hours) + Dec (degrees), or a planet by name (RA/Dec then ignored, the disc comes from its ephemeris). Ø = apparent size in degrees for an extended object (optional). Uncheck to skip one without deleting it. Saved with the rest of Settings.
